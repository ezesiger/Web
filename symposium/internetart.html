Internet Distribution to Display Art

Art made specifically for the web often relies on the pre-established methods of online distribution. This allows web-based art to utilize these structures in interesting ways (Olia Lialina).

Internet-based art also subverts the norms of art display (eg. museums and galleries) while making art more accessible (*assuming the viewer already has access to the internet)

  • Free access to art
  • Engages with people in a language most are familiar with (using their computer)
  • Removes physical barriers of entry
    • Not in a physical space. no need to travel.

Example

Dennis Cooper - GIF novels

GIFs stacked into chapter-long sequences for the user to interpret narratives. Utilizing poor media and the circulatory nature of GIFs

  • Must be downloaded to be engaged with (internet distribution channel)
